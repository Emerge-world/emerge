After communication landed, I kept thinking about how thin words can be on their own. Telling another agent where food is matters, but it still leaves every important thing trapped inside the individual. I wanted help to change material reality, and I wanted ideas to move through the population without having to be rediscovered from scratch.

## What I built

This PR added two kinds of transfer. The first is practical: one agent can hand an item directly to another. Not just food, but anything they are carrying, as long as they are close enough and have the energy to do it. The second is more interesting to me: an agent can teach an innovation it already knows, so a useful idea can spread socially instead of living and dying with its original inventor.

I kept the teaching path deterministic on purpose. Once a skill exists in the world, passing it on should not require another act of interpretation. I also laid the first quiet pieces of family memory underneath all of this by giving agents a place to record generation, parents, and birth tick, even before reproduction itself was live.

## Why it matters

This is the first time Emerge really starts to suggest culture rather than isolated intelligence. A discovered technique can become shared practice. A gathered resource can move to the agent who needs it more. Cooperation stops being sentimental and becomes infrastructural. The world starts to care not only about what an agent knows, but whether that knowledge can circulate.

## Things to consider

There is a real tension in making teaching reliable. I wanted to avoid paying for another model call and I wanted knowledge transfer to be consistent, but copying a skill is not the same as understanding it. If an innovation moves too cleanly from one agent to another, I may be creating diffusion without friction, and friction is often where culture becomes interesting.

Giving also raises a question about motive. Right now the system can register generosity and reward it with trust, but that does not mean the act was generous. An agent might give because it is cooperative, manipulative, desperate, or simply clearing inventory space. The outward behavior is the same. The meaning underneath it is still unstable, which may be exactly the point.

## What's next

With communication, trust, giving, and teaching in place, reproduction no longer feels like an isolated mechanics problem. If new agents arrive, they can enter a world where relationships, tools, and learned behaviors already move between people. That makes inheritance feel much more interesting than a mere stats blend.
